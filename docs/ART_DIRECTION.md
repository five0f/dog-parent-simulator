# Art direction

Version: 1.0

## Project identity

This is not a pet simulator.

This is not a cute corgi game.

This is not a cozy anime game.

This is a game about an ordinary person and their corgi living an ordinary life in Russia.

The game should feel recognizable, relatable, and slightly absurd.

The player should constantly think: "I have seen this in real life."

## Core emotional pillars

### Relatable

The world should feel familiar. The player should recognize:

- homes
- parks
- objects
- situations
- people

### Imperfect

Nothing should feel ideal. The world is lived in.

- Objects have history.
- Rooms have clutter.
- Parks have flaws.
- People are tired.
- Dogs are weird.

### Funny

Humor comes from observation.

- Not jokes.
- Not memes.
- Not references.
- Funny because it is true.

### Warm

The game should never feel cynical.

Even messy situations should feel human.

The relationship between the player and Bublik is always the emotional center.

## Visual references

Primary references:

- Russian web comics
- Everyday sketchbooks
- Hand-drawn illustrations
- Slice-of-life cartoons
- Observational humor

Avoid:

- anime
- Disney
- Pixar
- fantasy worlds
- futuristic environments
- polished mobile game aesthetics

## Environment storytelling

Every location should tell a story without text.

The player should understand who lives here, what happened here, and what kind of person owns this
dog.

## Home

The home should feel lived in. It should not be dirty, abandoned, minimalist, designer, or staged.

Possible details:

- package with packages
- dog leash hanging near door
- old sneakers
- tea mug
- receipts
- dog toys
- blanket on sofa
- grocery bag
- charging cable
- notes on refrigerator
- sock under furniture

Everything should feel accidental.

## Park

The park should feel local: not beautiful, not ugly, normal.

Possible details:

- pigeons
- benches
- dog owners
- trash bin
- uneven paths
- playground
- bushes
- random litter
- forgotten objects

The park should feel used.

## Character direction

### Player

The player character is an ordinary person. Not a hero, not stylish, not attractive by design.

Visual qualities:

- slightly tired
- casual clothing
- simple silhouette
- relatable appearance

The player should look like someone who actually owns a dog.

### Bublik

Bublik is the main character, not the player.

Bublik must be expressive. He should look curious, stubborn, emotional, and occasionally ridiculous.

Some poses should feel smart. Some poses should feel incredibly stupid. That contrast is important.

## Object design

Objects should feel real. Avoid fantasy props, decorative clutter, and random visual noise.

Every object should support storytelling. If an object exists, it should suggest a story.

## Humor direction

Humor comes from:

- dogs behaving like dogs
- people behaving like people
- small misunderstandings
- daily life

Avoid:

- internet memes
- random absurdism
- reference humor
- pop culture jokes

Preferred: observation-based humor.

## Writing examples

Good:

> Bublik brought a sock.
>
> He appears extremely proud of this achievement.

Good:

> Bublik has been staring at the door for fifteen minutes.
>
> This may be a request.
>
> Or a threat.

Bad:

> Bublik is happy.

Bad:

> LOL look at this funny dog.

## Scene composition

The environment provides context. The characters provide emotion. The event provides narrative. The
UI provides interaction.

In that order: environment first, interface last.

## Long-term goal

The player should not remember statistics. The player should remember stories and talk about
moments.

Examples:

- "Bublik stole my sock."
- "Bublik got into an argument with another dog."
- "Bublik refused to leave the park."

Those moments are the product.
